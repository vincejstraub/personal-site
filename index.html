I am a researcher and visual artist currently working at the Alan Turing Institute in the Public Policy Programme led by Helen Margetts. I am interested in understanding the development of collective intelligence and exploring how to best combine human and machine intelligence to address complex societal challenges. My research integrates computational social science with social pscyhology and public policy.

I have been fortunate to work with exceptional researchers in the past. After undergraduate studies at King's College London, I studied at the Oxford Internet Institute where I was advised by Taha Yasseri. I also interned at the Nesta Centre for Collective Intelligence Design where I worked with Geoff Mulgan and later supported a project led by Max Wolf in the Science of Intelligence joint Cluster of Excellence

My artistic works have been exhibited at galleries including Tate Modern and the Stedelijk Museum. Public engagement is also a part of my practice; I have previously been an ambassador for the Jubel European Democracy Festival and a member of the standing committee for the European Forum Alpbach.
